Through focusing on the environmental issue of fast fashion waste, I explored how type could be used as a metaphor for different concepts such as waste build up in landfills, cheap prices at the cost of quality, pollution, and overconsumption. I first explored the subject through individual letter studies using Museo Sans Display and Baskerville as my font choices and then translated some ideas generated into a poster covering the same issue.

The poster was produced by creating a quilt out of layered letters of various materials and pattern to create a three-dimensional background of vibrant clutter. The price tag placed on top was also physically created and printed in generic Helvetica and Alte Haas Grotesk to blend into the typology of fast fashion retail tags which are created with little thought or care. I then scanned the physical poster and digitally printed it to create a contrast to other flattened style posters and emphasize the materiality of the issue I was tackling.
